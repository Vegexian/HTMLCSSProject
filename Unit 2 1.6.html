Describing Accessibility Issues That Need Taking Into Account When Creating A Website

When it comes to accessibility for websites, businesses and companies have to cater to as many as they can, if not all of them. For people who are colour-blind, the websites would need to have the options to change the colour filter to make it easier to view the webpage.
An example that a colour-blind person could benefit from this is being able to navigate the webpages a lot easier, increasing the likeliness of their custom.

Another accessibility option that would be needed would be a setting that can increase the magnification of the page for those who have diminished sight. If the user was short sighted or long sighted, a magnification feature could help both cases by allowing the user to zoom in or zoom out of the webpage to suit their vision.
Another function would be a text-to-audio. This could also prove useful for those with difficulty seeing the webpage as it allows the user to hear what is written on the webpage instead of struggling to read it.

If a website has any audio/video content, a subtitle feature should be implemented for any customers that are hard of hearing or have completely lost their hearing. This will help them understand the website and help navigate around the website to find what they need.
The website would have to be written in a clear and concise manner to minimise all possibility of confusion for the user. This would mean to avoid using complex vocabulary, especially words that would require a dictionary to look up the meaning for.

Any pictures that are on the website would need to be coupled with a couple basic descriptive words to increase the ease of understanding the content on the webpage.
